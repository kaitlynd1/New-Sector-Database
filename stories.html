Alumni Stories

Jacquie Hanoman – LLF Fellow 2023–2024

Before LLF: Jacquie was the Executive Director of the Ross Community Center, leading transformational neighborhood revitalization work. Burnt out and struggling with joy in her work, she was encouraged by a foundation leader to explore the fellowship.

Why She Joined: “What really attracted me was that the fellowship stressed wellness and leadership. It sounded very promising, and that's what I found.”

Impact: The fellowship gave her tools to grow, reflect, and sustain herself as a leader. “At the beginning, we were told to imagine the most whole version of ourselves. That really marked the path for me.”

“It really did change my life. At the end of the day, if we are not well, we cannot do our work well.”

Corey Depina – LLF Fellow, Zumix, Boston

Before LLF: Corey had been leading music and youth empowerment work at Zumix, but hadn’t invested in his own development or well-being.

Transformation: “It reminded me to take care of myself. Now we do personal check-ins at work. Sometimes we skip meetings and get ice cream instead.”

Fellowship Experience: Corey loved the alumni retreat and workshops. “It was revolutionary to be in a space where people cared not just about work, but about each other.”

“The New Sector approach is about the individual. Even when I leave my role, I’ll still be someone who invests in myself.”
